The Workflow

1

Source Material

Prioritize video with clear distinction between subject and environment. High-contrast footage ensures the Roto Brush efficiently tracks edge data.

2

Technical Splicing

Using CapCut, technically splice the source footage into clean 5-second segments to manage rendering load and maintain stability in After Effects.

3

Layer Compositing

Isolate the subject layer utilizing the refined Roto Brush workflow, freezing frames iteratively to lock the edge data.

WK 01

Asset Prep

Securing footage and preparing the 60-second sequence for the authoring environment.

WK 02

Base Roto

Beginning initial brush passes in AE for the first 10 seconds to lock the baseline rhythm.

WK 03

Stylization

Establishing the base background and testing solid fill techniques for style definition.

WK 04

Refinement

Smoothing edge chatter from initial brush passes to secure a cleaner matte.

WK 05

Core Masking

Applying rigid masking to stubborn background elements bleeding into the subject frame.

WK 06

Edge Detection

Refining fine details and complex object shapes throughout the middle timeline sequence.

WK 07

Motion Blur

Introducing artificial motion blur to isolated subjects to match original camera tracking.

WK 08

Subject Tracking

Finalizing the rotoscope passes for the remaining frames and ensuring solid matte consistency.

WK 09

Color Grading

Applying final color correction to pop the subject out visually from the new background.

WK 10

Environment Sync

Ensuring all environmental shadows and light wraps look native to the new composite.

WK 11

Final Render Prep

Running low-res test exports to catch any final macro-chatter or dropped masks.

WK 12

Sequence Delivery

Exporting the master 60-second clip in maximum presentation quality for the portfolio.
